HOW CAN WE MAKE
CONTEXT VISIBLE?

Zac & Research Lab Team
NYU Tandon School of Engineering • Research Excellence

The Challenge

LLMs are effectively stateless. Context is implicit, hidden within winding conversation histories that are difficult for users to inspect or control.

"Current interfaces force a trade-off: invisible context leads to model failure, while full visibility leads to cognitive overload."

Context Rot

Empirical evidence (Chroma, 2025) shows frontier models degrade as context grows. Attention dilution and "lost in the middle" effects are systematic failures of unstructured context delivery.

Cognitive Dependency

When AI manages context invisibly, users drift into "instruct, serve, repeat" loops. This limits deep cognitive engagement and fosters unhealthy AI codependency.

The Framework: Spatial Metaphors

We propose that navigable spatial metaphors (like transit maps) allow users to externalize project knowledge as a structured graph.

By mapping context explicitly, the user transitions from a passive consumer of AI outputs to an active architect of the model's epistemic state.

Research Gap

Most existing research treats context as a technical bottleneck to be solved via compression or automated RAG. We identify a gap in Interactional Context Engineering: using UI to facilitate shared human-AI understanding.

Early Findings

  • Reduced "re-explanation" loops in complex coding tasks.
  • Slower, more intentional prompting (Reduced Codependency).
  • Higher user confidence in model reasoning steps.
-23% Context Regret
+40% Model Trust

Conclusion

Visible context is the antidote to AI-led cognitive atrophy. When we can see the lines and stops of our ideas, we can lead the model, rather than following it.
